Panipuri

Small, crispy puris filled with spicy water, tangy tamarind chutney, and potatoes â€“ a beloved street food all over India!

Ingredients

  • For the Pani (spicy water):
  • 1 cup mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1 tbsp tamarind pulp
  • 1 tsp black salt
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1 tsp chat masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 cups chilled water
  • For the filling:
  • 2 boiled potatoes, mashed
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1/4 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/4 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/4 tsp black salt
  • Salt to taste
  • For assembling:
  • 20-25 panipuri puris
  • Tamarind chutney
  • Chopped coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. To prepare the pani, blend mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, tamarind pulp, black salt, roasted cumin powder, chat masala, and salt with chilled water in a blender.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the pulp and refrigerate the water for at least 30 minutes.
  3. To prepare the filling, mash the boiled potatoes and mix with boiled chickpeas, cumin powder, red chili powder, black salt, and regular salt.
  4. To assemble, gently poke a hole in the center of each puri and stuff it with the potato and chickpea mixture.
  5. Dip each filled puri into the chilled spicy pani and serve immediately. Garnish with tamarind chutney and chopped coriander leaves.
  6. Enjoy your refreshing and delicious panipuri!
