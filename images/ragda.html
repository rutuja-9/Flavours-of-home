Ragda Pattice

Soft, spiced potato patties served with a flavorful white pea curry – a famous Mumbai street food!

Ingredients

  • For the Ragda (white pea curry):
  • 1 cup dried white peas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • For the Pattice (potato patties):
  • 3 large boiled potatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/4 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for shallow frying
  • For garnishing:
  • Tamarind chutney
  • Green chutney
  • Chopped onions
  • Chopped coriander leaves
  • Sev (crispy chickpea noodles)

Instructions

  1. To prepare the ragda, cook soaked white peas in a pressure cooker with water until soft (around 3-4 whistles).
  2. In a pan, heat some oil and sauté chopped onions, ginger-garlic paste, and tomatoes until soft. Add turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
  3. Add the cooked peas and some water to adjust the consistency. Let it simmer for 10 minutes and set aside.
  4. To prepare the pattice, mash the boiled potatoes and mix with onions, coriander leaves, cumin powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
  5. Shape the mixture into small round patties and shallow fry in a pan until golden brown on both sides.
  6. To serve, place the pattice on a plate, pour the hot ragda over them, and garnish with tamarind chutney, green chutney, onions, coriander leaves, and sev.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy!
